Dak Dey Tut (Dak Otong Tut)

Lou Nuer pioneer — early learner from Khartoum, named Nuer sections, and historical proponent of the name Jonglei.

Also known as Dak Otong Tut. Brother: Gai Otong Tut. Mother: Joy Tiap.

Biography

Born in the Lou Nuer community, Dak Dey Tut (also recorded as Dak Otong Tut) is widely remembered in local oral history as one of the first from his people to pursue formal learning in Khartoum in the early 1900s. His family connections — including his older brother Gai Otong Tut and their mother Joy Tiap — are preserved in community memory. He is credited with naming the Nuer sections and advocating the use of the name Jonglei for the larger region.

Why this site

This site exists to collect testimonies, images, documents, and community knowledge about Dak Dey Tut: the person, his family, and his contributions to Nuer identity and regional naming.
